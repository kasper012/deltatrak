Frederick Wu

President and Company Founder

Frederick Wu

President and Company Founder

Fred Wu founded DeltaTrak, Inc. in 1989 and is a pioneer and entrepreneur in developing cold chain and food safety solutions.

Mr. Wu holds multiple patents related to unique technology features, including a single use, in-transit temperature recorder that was the first product that launched DeltaTrak back in 1989, and to this date it is still tracking over a million trips per year.

Mr. Wu has a diverse background in engineering, product development, clinical trials, international sales and marketing working in the medical device and instrumentation field for American Hospital Supply , IVAC Corporation, a pioneer of clinical electronic thermometers, and Camino Laboratories, a fiber optic medical technology startup company, prior to founding DeltaTrak.

Mr. Wu attended the University of Southern California and holds a BS in Industrial Engineering and a Masters in Finance.

Ray Caron

Chief Operating Officer

Ray Caron

Chief Operating Officer

Ray joined DeltaTrak in 2010 as Marketing Manager and currently holds the position of Chief Operating Officer.

Mr. Caron is an experienced executive and has held multiple positions as Vice President in Marketing and Vice President in Sales. Prior to DeltaTrak, Mr. Caron was President and part owner of a supplies distribution company, and he has held various positions at Hewlett-Packard, Inc., Fairchild/Sentry Test System, Ungermann-Bass, Inc., and four silicon valley startups.

Mr. Caron attended San Jose State University in San Jose California and has B.S. degrees in Aeronautics and Computer Science.

Steve Stringer

Chief Financial Officer

Steve Stringer

Chief Financial Officer

Steve joined DeltaTrak in 2020 in the position of Chief Financial Officer for the DeltaTrak Corporate Group. He has previously worked for DeltaTrak in 1998 - 2000 as the Vice President of Finance and Administration.

Mr. Stringer is an experienced financial executive and has held the Chief Financial Officer position in a diverse group of public and private organizations in the medical device, software and high-tech industries with significant international operations and M&A growth strategies. Mr. Stringer’s company portfolio included divisions of Eastman Kodak, Corning and Eli Lilly as well as PE backed mid-size, high-growth organizations.

Mr. Stringer attended the University of Southern California and holds a Master of Business Administration and a BBA in Accounting from Boise State University.

Benlih Huang
